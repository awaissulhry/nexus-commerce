## Plain English

The cost figure can now tell the truth about HOW. Enter what a jacket really draws — leather m² per size (+ wastage %), labor hours, and three global rates (leather €/m², labor €/h, overhead %) — and the Owner-only line card stops showing one opaque cost number and shows the recipe: "Material (2.4 m² +8% waste) €129.60 · Labor (6h × €20.00) €120.00 · Overhead 15% €37.44". Enter nothing (today) and nothing changes, to the cent. The estimate now also meets reality: once a converted order SHIPS, its actual ledger cost appears beside the quote's estimate — on the converted quote itself, on "repeat" rows in the similar-quotes card ("won at €X · actual cost €Y vs est €Z"), and as a dismissible "last time" hint when you quote a garment this party already bought. And the promise date stops being a blind default: a suggestion line shows the honest formula — "3w base + 1.6w backlog + 2w leather" — computed from the real work-order queue and real leather stock, with an Apply button. It never writes by itself; the Owner stays in control of every number.

## Choices flagged (per the phase brief)

- **Base lead lives at `production.leadTimeDays`**, not inside pricing.defaults as the brief assumed — the FP3 key quote-creation already reads; the suggestion uses the same source so parity is literal. The NEW keys went into pricing.defaults as briefed.
- **`@@unique([templateId, sizeKey])` can't stop two null-size rows on SQLite** (NULLs are distinct) — the costing PUT enforces at-most-one all-sizes row at the boundary instead.
- **Size-run lines resolve to the null-size row** (a run spans sizes; one line has one per-unit cost) — per-size cost precision is EPD's editor territory.

Flagged as the deviation it is.

Both remain open for a later EPQ phase gate.
