## What this changes in the ERP (binding on EPF.1/EPF.4/EPF.7 specs)

1. **Posture A hardened:** the PDF must be relabeled "**Copia di cortesia — documento privo di valore fiscale**" (or Proforma) AND the ERP must **never print a definitive invoice number** that the external legal issuer might not reproduce. Instead: EPF.3/EPF.4 add a reconciliation field storing the externally-issued invoice number + SDI identificativo/date against the order — otherwise TD24 summaries can't be tied to DDTs.
2. **D-05 defect reworded:** the live `INV-n` numbering is *legally* acceptable (progressive + unique); the P0 remains for the **non-atomic mint** (burned numbers, collision 500s) and the **absent credit-note document**. Per-year `INV-2026-n` stays recommended (clean cutover, posture-B/C-ready), no longer justified as "required by law."
3. **DDT support is load-bearing** for any differita path: delivery documents need date, progressive number, parties, goods description; each TD24 references its DDT numbers/dates. (DDT surface belongs to Shipping/EPS — registry note if posture B/C is chosen.)
4. **Bollo logic narrows:** intra-EU (N3.2) and true-export (N3.1) lines are bollo-exempt — for this factory's mix, bollo will be rare; the flag + quarterly dashboard only matter under posture B/C.
5. **VIES gate:** EU zero-rating requires the stored VIES check (timestamp + result) — already owned by EPQ.5's tax-mode substrate; EPF consumes, and the invoice line must carry the art. 41 citation text.
6. **Deadline timers (EPF D-2):** under posture B/C, the 12-day (TD01) and 15th-of-month (TD24) clocks become hard scheduler deadlines with scarto-retry inside 5 effective days; under posture A they are the *commercialista's* deadlines — the ERP's monthly close checklist just reminds.
7. **Cost of posture C is now known:** ~€19.90/month (A-Cube tier) at this volume — cheap enough that the A→C revisit threshold is operational (who does the work), not financial.
